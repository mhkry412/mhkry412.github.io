Research internship project at BRIN investigating the topological
properties of **stanene** (2D tin, a buckled honeycomb lattice)
using first-principles DFT.

Stanene is a topological insulator predicted to host quantum spin
Hall edge states, making it promising for low-power electronics
and spintronics.

### Methods

- **Quantum ESPRESSO** — fully relativistic DFT with SOC
- PAW pseudopotentials (PBE and PBEsol)
- Band structure, DOS, and projected DOS analysis
- Edge state calculations

### Results

- Computed electronic band structure with and without SOC
- Identified topological band inversion at the $\Gamma$ point
- Analyzed spin-texture of edge states
