Research internship at ITS Physics Department / G'nomaC (PT. Graphene
Nanomaterial Consulting) focused on developing a sustainable synthesis
route for graphene oxide (GO) from **coconut fiber biomass**.

### Motivation

Conventional GO synthesis relies on graphite, a non-renewable
resource. Coconut fiber — abundant agricultural waste in Indonesia —
offers a renewable carbon source for graphene derivatives.

### Methods

- Biomass pre-treatment and carbonization
- Chemical oxidation (modified Hummers method)
- Exfoliation and purification
- Characterization (XRD, FTIR, Raman, SEM)

### Outcomes

- Successfully produced few-layer GO from coconut fiber
- Comparable quality to graphite-derived GO
- Contributed to sustainable nanomaterials research in Indonesia
