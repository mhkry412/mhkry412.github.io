I'm **Hanandaru Mahaputra Purwanto** — a computational physicist and
research assistant at **BRIN (National Research and Innovation Agency
of Indonesia)**, Pusat Riset Fisika Kuantum, and an M.Sc. candidate in
Physics at **Universitas Indonesia**.

My research focuses on **first-principles and atomistic simulations**
of two-dimensional materials. I use **Density Functional Theory**
(Quantum ESPRESSO) and **Molecular Dynamics** (LAMMPS/ReaxFF) to
investigate electronic, magnetic, and structural properties of low-
dimensional systems — from hexagonal boron nitride and stanene to
MoS₂ growth on mica substrates.

### Research approach

I believe in the **Dead Simple Sites** philosophy applied to research:
clean, reproducible workflows; minimal but meaningful analysis; and
clear communication of results. Whether I'm running DFT on a
supercomputer or analyzing MD trajectories, my goal is the same:
*less, but better.*

### Beyond research

When I'm not on the command line, I enjoy reading about quantum
materials, exploring new simulation methods, and contributing to
open-source scientific software.
